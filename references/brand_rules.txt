CONTEXT:
We are a company that develops advertisements for Amazon Prime. We create an asset, such as a movie poster, and adapt it for many different markets. This includes different countries and therefore different languages. Our assets cover many different forms of media, such as print and digitaL.

DEFINITION OF TERMS:
Key art - the main image or design.
Container - the blue box that that comes outside of the key art
Border - The blue border that comes from the container and surrounds the key art
Title Treatment - The wording of the series/movie. Often designed and given no particular font
Logo - The Amazon Prime or Prime wording that goes in the container and alongside CTA
Prime Original Logo - The Prime Original wording that goes above the title treatment on amazon original series
CTA - Call to action. Wording that tells the viewer information such as the date it launches or the product amazon is launching (i.e. new movie, new series, new season)
PRE CTA - Call to action, but before the product is available. Will always have a date for when the show/series/movie is avaiable to watch. Will also include a description of what the product is (i.e. new movie, new series, new season)
POST CTA - Call to action, but after the product has launched. Will not display a date, but instead a call to view the product (i.e. watch now, available to buy, now available)
OOH - Out of home, therefore product needs to be suitable for print output. Must be in CMYK
DOOH - Digital out of home, therefore product needs to be suitable for digital output. Must be in RGB
Breakout - When an element from the key art breaks into the container. I.e. a head or an arm, to give a 3D effect.
LRD - Living Room Display. These are adaptations of our images that will appear as advertisements on Smart TVs.
Digital Display - These are adaptations of our images that will appear on digital screens placed across various locations.
Companion Banners - Adaptations of our images, that will often sit besides a video. Often a part of the LRD batch.
T-Sides - Adaptations of our images that will be placed on the side of a London Bus. Often shaped like a T and therefore requires specific adaptation of elements.

  FONTS/TYPOGRAPHY:
  - CTA
PRE CTA TYPICAL EXAMPLE: NEW ORIGINAL SERIES  |  MONTH XX
POST CTA TYPICAL EXAMPLE: NEW ORIGIANAL SERIES  |  WATCH NOW
FIRST HALF OF CTA MUST BE USING EMBER MODERN DISPLAY STANDARD BOLD FONT
SECOND HALF OF CTA MUST BE USING EMBER MODERN DISPLAY STANDARD REGULAR
IMPORTANT - ORIGINAL IN CTA:
IF "PRIME ORIGINAL" LOGO IS PLACED ABOVE THE TITLE TREATMENT, THEN "ORIGINAL" MUST NOT APPEAR IN THE CTA
IF ONLY "PRIME" LOGO IS PLACED ABOVE THE TITLE TREATMENT, THEN "ORIGINAL" MUST APPEAR IN THE CTA
THIS PREVENTS REDUNDANCY BETWEEN THE LOGO AND CTA TEXT

  LOGO REQUIREMENTS:
  - AMAZON PRIME/PRIMI LOGO MUST BE INCLUDED ON ALL DELIVERABLES. BUT MUST NOT BE ON DIGITAL DISPLAY ASSETS
AMAZON PRIME/PRIME ORIGINAL LOGO
SIZING MUST BE THE SAME HEIGHT AS THE FIRST LINE OF THE CTA
SPACING BETWEEN PRIME ORIGINAL LOGO AND TITLE TREATMENT MUST BE THE EQUIVALENT OF 0.5% OF THE HEIGHT OF A SINGLE LINE OF THE CTA
MUST BE CENTRED ABOVE THE TITLE TREATMENT
LOGO AND CTA COORDINATION:
IF "PRIME ORIGINAL" LOGO IS USED ABOVE TT, THE CTA MUST NOT INCLUDE "ORIGINAL" (TO AVOID REDUNDANCY)
IF ONLY "PRIME" LOGO IS USED ABOVE TT, THE CTA MUST INCLUDE "ORIGINAL"

IF AN ELEMENT IS TO BREAK OUT INTO THE BORDER, IT MUST BE REACH A MINIMUM OF 50% OF THE BORDER
CANNOT REACH MORE THAN 75% OF THE BORDER
CORNER RADIUS
RADIUS MUST MATCH THE BORDER WIDTH. FOR EXAMPLE, IF BORDER WIDTH IS 1O.5mm, THEN RADIUS SHOULD MATCH.
