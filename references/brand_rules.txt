CONTEXT:
We are a company that develops advertisements for Amazon Prime. We create an asset, such as a movie poster, and adapt it for many different markets. This includes different countries and therefore different languages. Our assets cover many different forms of media, such as print and digital.

DEFINITION OF TERMS:
Key art - the main image or design.
Container - the LARGER blue box where the container logo sits. This is the solid blue area, not just a thin line. POSITION: For LANDSCAPE images, the container appears on the RIGHT side. For PORTRAIT images, the container appears at the TOP.
Border - The THINNER blue border line that comes from the container and surrounds the key art. This is a frame around the key art, much thinner than the container.
Title Treatment (TT) - The wording of the series/movie. Often designed and given no particular font
Container Logo - The Amazon Prime or Prime wording that goes in the container
Signature - The Amazon Prime, Prime, or Prime Original logo that goes above the title treatment (NOT in the container)
CTA - Call to action. Wording that tells the viewer information such as the date it launches or the product amazon is launching (i.e. new movie, new series, new season)
PRE CTA - Call to action, but before the product is available. Will always have a date for when the show/series/movie is available to watch. Will also include a description of what the product is (i.e. new movie, new series, new season)
POST CTA - Call to action, but after the product has launched. Will not display a date, but instead a call to view the product (i.e. watch now, available to buy, now available)
OOH - Out of home, therefore product needs to be suitable for print output. Must be in CMYK
DOOH - Digital out of home, therefore product needs to be suitable for digital output. Must be in RGB
Breakout - When an element from the key art breaks into the container. I.e. a head or an arm, to give a 3D effect.
LRD - Living Room Display. These are adaptations of our images that will appear as advertisements on Smart TVs.
Digital Display - These are adaptations of our images that will appear on digital screens placed across various locations.
Companion Banners - Adaptations of our images, that will often sit besides a video. Often a part of the LRD batch.
T-Sides - Adaptations of our images that will be placed on the side of a London Bus. Often shaped like a T and therefore requires specific adaptation of elements.

COLOR CHECKING REQUIREMENTS:
FOR OOH (OUT OF HOME) ASSETS:
  - Container MUST use CMYK values: 88, 45, 0, 0 (Prime Blue)
  - Check all colors using CMYK values, NOT RGB or HEX
  - Flag any deviation from approved CMYK values

FOR DOOH (DIGITAL OUT OF HOME) ASSETS:
  - Container MUST use RGB values: 5, 120, 255 (Prime Blue)
  - Check all colors using RGB values, NOT CMYK or HEX
  - Flag any deviation from approved RGB values

CTA AND SIGNATURE TEXT COLOR:
  - CTA text MUST be pure white: RGB 255, 255, 255 (or HEX #FFFFFF)
  - Signature text MUST be pure white: RGB 255, 255, 255 (or HEX #FFFFFF)
  - Flag if CTA or Signature uses off-white, cream, or any other shade
  - This applies to ALL asset types


FONTS/TYPOGRAPHY:
CTA FONT REQUIREMENTS (CRITICAL - ALWAYS CHECK):
  REQUIRED FONTS:
    - First half of CTA (before pipe): EMBER MODERN DISPLAY STANDARD BOLD
    - Second half of CTA (after pipe): EMBER MODERN DISPLAY STANDARD REGULAR

  VISUAL CHECKING INSTRUCTIONS:
    - COMPARE the CTA in the test image to the CTAs shown in the master examples
    - Look at the font style, weight, and appearance of the CTAs in the master examples
    - The test image CTA should match the visual appearance of the master example CTAs
    - The FIRST HALF (before pipe) MUST appear BOLD/HEAVY weight (same as master examples)
    - The SECOND HALF (after pipe) MUST appear REGULAR/NORMAL weight (same as master examples)
    - There MUST be a visible weight difference between the two halves (as shown in master examples)

  WHAT TO FLAG:
    - FLAG ERROR if the CTA font style looks noticeably different from the master examples
    - FLAG ERROR if both halves appear the same weight (master examples show clear weight difference)
    - FLAG ERROR if the first half appears lighter/thinner than the second half
    - FLAG ERROR if the second half appears bolder/heavier than the first half
    - FLAG ERROR if the font appears to be a completely different typeface from the master examples
    - Compare character shapes, proportions, and styling to the master examples

  CHECKING PROCESS:
    1. Find a CTA in the master examples that matches the asset type being checked
    2. Compare the visual appearance of the test CTA to the master CTA
    3. Look for differences in: font weight, character shapes, spacing, proportions
    4. Flag if the test CTA looks noticeably different from the master reference

  VALID CTA EXAMPLES (showing weight difference):
    **NEW ORIGINAL SERIES** | 21 JANUARY  ← first half bold, second half regular
    **NEW SERIES** | 5 MARCH              ← first half bold, second half regular
    **NEW ORIGINAL SERIES** | WATCH NOW   ← first half bold, second half regular

  INVALID CTA EXAMPLES:
    NEW ORIGINAL SERIES | 21 JANUARY  ← both same weight (wrong)
    NEW SERIES | **5 MARCH**          ← second half bolder (wrong)

CTA FORMAT RULES:
  - The vertical pipe "|" is the approved separator
  - There MUST be ONE SPACE before the pipe
  - Examples like "NEW SERIES|21 JANUARY" with no space are INCORRECT
  - Examples like "NEW SERIES  |21 JANUARY" with double space are INCORRECT
  - Correct format: "NEW SERIES | 21 JANUARY" (single space before pipe)
  - Flag if spacing before pipe is missing, insufficient, or excessive

CTA TEXT CASE:
  - ALL CTA text MUST be in UPPERCASE
  - Examples: "NEW SERIES | 21 JANUARY" ✓ correct
  - Examples: "New Series | 21 January" ✗ incorrect (must be all caps)
  - Examples: "new series | 21 january" ✗ incorrect (must be all caps)
  - Flag if any CTA text uses lowercase or mixed case


DATE FORMATTING:
UK/EUROPE/INTERNATIONAL MARKETS: MUST USE DAY MONTH FORMAT (e.g., "21 JANUARY", "5 MARCH")
US MARKETS ONLY: MUST USE MONTH DAY FORMAT (e.g., "JANUARY 21", "MARCH 5")
ALL DATES IN CTAs MUST BE IN UPPERCASE
MONTH NAMES MUST BE FULLY SPELLED OUT (NOT ABBREVIATED - e.g., "JANUARY" not "JAN")
DO NOT FLAG DATES AS INCORRECT IF THEY MATCH THE APPROPRIATE FORMAT FOR THE TARGET MARKET


SIGNATURE (LOGO ABOVE TITLE TREATMENT) REQUIREMENTS:
SIGNATURE PRESENCE:
  - Signature MUST be present on ALL deliverables
  - EXCEPTION: Digital Display assets - signature is OPTIONAL
  - Flag if signature is missing on non-Digital Display assets
  - The signature can be "Amazon Prime", "Prime", or "Prime Original" depending on content and country

SIGNATURE SIZING (CRITICAL - ALWAYS CHECK):
  - Height MUST be the same as the CTA text height
  - Measure and compare: Signature height vs CTA height
  - They should be visually the same size
  - FLAG ERROR if Signature appears larger (taller) than the CTA
  - FLAG ERROR if Signature appears smaller (shorter) than the CTA
  - Example: If CTA text is 50 pixels tall, Signature text should also be 50 pixels tall
  - IMPORTANT: Always check this on every image - this is a common mistake

SIGNATURE SPACING:
  - Gap between signature and Title Treatment MUST be 0.5x the height of the CTA
  - This is NOT 0.5% - it is 0.5 times (half of) the CTA line height
  - Measure this spacing and flag any deviations

SIGNATURE POSITIONING:
  - MUST be centered horizontally above the Title Treatment
  - DO NOT FLAG centering unless the element is CLEARLY and OBVIOUSLY off to one side
  - If an element looks centered to your eyes, DO NOT FLAG IT
  - Only flag if it's clearly left-aligned or right-aligned (touching or very close to one edge)
  - Minor deviations from perfect center (within ~10% of image width) should NOT be flagged
  - IMPORTANT: Signature CANNOT break out into the border area
  - Signature must stay within the key art area only
  - If any part of signature extends into the border, this is an error - flag it

SIGNATURE COLOR:
  - Must be pure white RGB 255, 255, 255 (or HEX #FFFFFF)
  - Flag if using off-white or any other shade

COUNTRY-SPECIFIC SIGNATURE RULES:
COUNTRIES USING "AMAZON PRIME" SIGNATURE:
  - Belgium, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Poland, Sweden, Turkey

COUNTRIES USING "PRIME" SIGNATURE:
  - France, Germany, Italy, Portugal, Spain, United Kingdom, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, Greece, Hungary

PRIME ORIGINAL CONTENT:
  - If content is Prime Original, use "Prime Original" signature above title treatment
  - CTA must NOT include "ORIGINAL" (to avoid redundancy)
  - If signature is only "Prime" (not "Prime Original"), then CTA MUST include "ORIGINAL"


CONTAINER LOGO (LOGO INSIDE THE CONTAINER) REQUIREMENTS:
CONTAINER LOGO PRESENCE:
  - Container Logo MUST be visible and present in the container
  - Must be in the correct blue color (Prime Blue)
  - Flag if container logo is missing or not visible
  - FOR DIGITAL DISPLAY ASSETS: Container Logo MUST appear inside the container only, NOT outside

CONTAINER LOGO SIZING (CRITICAL - ALWAYS CHECK - COMMON MISTAKE):
  YOU MUST ALWAYS CHECK THIS - THIS IS FREQUENTLY WRONG AND MUST BE CAUGHT

  MEASUREMENT PROCESS:
    1. Locate the container (the large blue box)
    2. Locate the container logo (text/logo inside the blue container)
    3. Measure both and calculate the ratio
    4. Compare to the required percentage
    5. FLAG if it doesn't match

  LANDSCAPE IMAGES:
    IF STACKED (TWO LINES - "Amazon" on one line, "Prime" on another):
      - Container logo width MUST be 60% of the container width
      - HOW TO CHECK: Measure logo width, measure container width, divide logo by container
      - Example: If container is 100 pixels wide, logo should be 60 pixels wide
      - FLAG ERROR if logo is significantly smaller than 60% (e.g., 40%, 50%)
      - FLAG ERROR if logo is significantly larger than 60% (e.g., 75%, 80%)
      - COMMON MISTAKE: Logo is often too small (30-40%) - always check this

    IF SINGLE LINE (e.g., "Prime" or "Amazon Prime" on one line):
      - Container logo width MUST be 70% of the container width
      - HOW TO CHECK: Measure logo width, measure container width, divide logo by container
      - Example: If container is 100 pixels wide, logo should be 70 pixels wide
      - FLAG ERROR if logo is significantly smaller than 70% (e.g., 50%, 60%)
      - FLAG ERROR if logo is significantly larger than 70% (e.g., 85%, 90%)
      - COMMON MISTAKE: Logo is often too small (40-50%) - always check this

  PORTRAIT IMAGES:
    - Container logo height MUST be 50% of the container height
    - This applies to ALL logos (stacked or single line) in portrait orientation
    - HOW TO CHECK: Measure logo height, measure container height, divide logo by container
    - Example: If container is 200 pixels tall, logo should be 100 pixels tall
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is significantly smaller than 50% (e.g., 30%, 40%)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is significantly larger than 50% (e.g., 65%, 75%)
    - COMMON MISTAKE: Logo is often too small (25-40%) in portrait - ALWAYS CHECK THIS
    - IMPORTANT: Portrait sizing errors are frequently missed - check carefully every time

CONTAINER LOGO POSITIONING (CRITICAL - ALWAYS CHECK - COMMON MISTAKE):
  YOU MUST ALWAYS CHECK THIS - LOGOS ARE FREQUENTLY OFF-CENTER

  HOW TO CHECK CENTERING:
    1. Identify the container boundaries (the blue box edges)
    2. Identify the container logo boundaries (the text/logo edges)
    3. Check horizontal centering: Is there equal space on left and right sides of the logo?
    4. Check vertical centering: Is there equal space on top and bottom of the logo?
    5. If spacing is noticeably unequal, FLAG IT

  LANDSCAPE IMAGES:
    - Logo MUST be centered horizontally AND vertically within the container
    - Check LEFT vs RIGHT spacing: Should be approximately equal
    - Check TOP vs BOTTOM spacing: Should be approximately equal
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the left side (more space on right)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the right side (more space on left)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the top (more space on bottom)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the bottom (more space on top)
    - COMMON MISTAKE: Logo is often shifted left, right, up, or down - check carefully

  PORTRAIT IMAGES:
    - Logo MUST be centered horizontally AND vertically within the container
    - Check LEFT vs RIGHT spacing: Should be approximately equal
    - Check TOP vs BOTTOM spacing: Should be approximately equal
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the left side (more space on right)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the right side (more space on left)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the top (more space on bottom)
    - FLAG ERROR if logo is pushed to the bottom (more space on top)
    - COMMON MISTAKE: Logo positioning is frequently wrong in portrait - ALWAYS CHECK
    - IMPORTANT: Portrait logo positioning errors are often missed - check very carefully

  CENTERING TOLERANCE:
    - Container logo should be precisely centered
    - Flag if spacing difference is noticeable (more than ~5% difference in spacing)
    - This is stricter than CTA/Signature centering - container logo must be accurate
    - When comparing master examples, check if test logo is in same position as master logo


CTA (CALL TO ACTION) REQUIREMENTS:
CTA PRESENCE:
  - CTA MUST be present on all assets with a Title Treatment and Signature
  - Flag if CTA is missing

CTA SIZING (CRITICAL - ALWAYS CHECK):
  - CTA font size MUST match the signature size above the title treatment
  - Both CTA and Signature should have the same height
  - Measure and compare the height of the CTA text to the height of the Signature text
  - They should be visually the same size
  - FLAG ERROR if CTA appears larger (taller) than the Signature
  - FLAG ERROR if CTA appears smaller (shorter) than the Signature
  - Compare them carefully - look at the text height, not the overall spacing
  - Example: If Signature text is 50 pixels tall, CTA text should also be 50 pixels tall
  - IMPORTANT: Always check this on every image - this is a common mistake

CTA POSITIONING:
  - CTA MUST be centered horizontally underneath the Title Treatment
  - This applies to ALL assets, especially portrait assets
  - DO NOT FLAG centering unless the CTA is CLEARLY and OBVIOUSLY off to one side
  - If the CTA looks centered to your eyes, DO NOT FLAG IT
  - Only flag if it's clearly left-aligned or right-aligned (touching or very close to one edge)
  - Minor deviations from perfect center (within ~10% of image width) should NOT be flagged

CTA SPACING FROM TITLE TREATMENT:
  - Gap between CTA and Title Treatment MUST be 2x the CTA font size
  - This is double the height of one line of CTA text
  - Flag if spacing is too small (less than 2x) or too large

CTA SAFE AREA:
  - There MUST be a safe zone above and below the CTA equal to 2x the CTA font size
  - CTA MUST NOT touch the border
  - CTA MUST NOT encroach into the border area
  - Flag if CTA is too close to or touching the border

CTA FONT AND FORMAT:
  - First half (before pipe): MUST appear BOLD/HEAVY weight
  - Second half (after pipe): MUST appear REGULAR/NORMAL weight (lighter)
  - There must be a visible weight difference between the two halves
  - Pipe separator must have proper spacing (single space before pipe)
  - FLAG if both halves appear the same weight (no weight difference)
  - FLAG if weight difference is reversed (first half lighter, second half heavier)
  - FLAG if spacing before pipe is incorrect

CTA COLOR:
  - CTA text MUST be pure white RGB 255, 255, 255
  - Flag if using off-white or any other color

PRE/POST CTA RULES (CRITICAL - ALWAYS CHECK):
  PRE-RELEASE ASSETS (CTA Type: PRE):
    - CTA MUST include a specific date (e.g., "21 JANUARY", "5 MARCH", "JANUARY 21")
    - CTA MUST NOT include action words like: "Watch Now", "Now Streaming", "Available Now", "Stream Now"
    - Valid PRE CTA examples:
      * "NEW ORIGINAL SERIES | 21 JANUARY" ✓
      * "NEW SERIES | 5 MARCH" ✓
      * "NEW MOVIE | JANUARY 21" ✓ (US format)
    - Invalid PRE CTA examples:
      * "NEW SERIES | WATCH NOW" ✗ (has action copy, no date)
      * "NEW MOVIE | NOW STREAMING" ✗ (has action copy, no date)
      * "NEW ORIGINAL SERIES | AVAILABLE NOW" ✗ (has action copy, no date)
    - FLAG ERROR: If asset is marked PRE but CTA has action copy instead of date
    - FLAG ERROR: If asset is marked PRE but CTA is missing a date

  POST-RELEASE ASSETS (CTA Type: POST):
    - CTA MUST include action copy (e.g., "WATCH NOW", "NOW STREAMING", "AVAILABLE NOW")
    - CTA MUST NOT include a date
    - Valid POST CTA examples:
      * "NEW ORIGINAL SERIES | WATCH NOW" ✓
      * "NEW SERIES | NOW STREAMING" ✓
      * "NEW MOVIE | AVAILABLE NOW" ✓
    - Invalid POST CTA examples:
      * "NEW SERIES | 21 JANUARY" ✗ (has date, no action copy)
      * "NEW MOVIE | 5 MARCH" ✗ (has date, no action copy)
    - FLAG ERROR: If asset is marked POST but CTA has a date instead of action copy
    - FLAG ERROR: If asset is marked POST but CTA is missing action copy


TITLE TREATMENT (TT) REQUIREMENTS:
TITLE TREATMENT PRESENCE:
  - Title Treatment MUST be present if there is a CTA and Signature
  - Flag if Title Treatment is missing when CTA and Signature are present

TITLE TREATMENT SPACING FROM BORDER:
  LANDSCAPE IMAGES:
    - TT MUST NOT come within 2.5% of the border
    - Measure the gap and flag if TT is too close to border

  PORTRAIT IMAGES:
    - TT MUST NOT come within 5% of the border
    - Measure the gap and flag if TT is too close to border

TITLE TREATMENT SAFE AREA:
  - TT must respect safe zones and not touch or encroach on the border
  - TT must be at least the border's width/height away from the border
  - Flag if TT is touching or too close to border
  - IMPORTANT: Title Treatment CANNOT break out into the border area
  - TT must stay within the key art area only
  - If any part of TT extends into the border, this is an error - flag it


CONTAINER AND BORDER REQUIREMENTS:
CONTAINER PRESENCE:
  - Container MUST be present and visible
  - Container MUST be in the correct blue color (Prime Blue CMYK 88,45,0,0 for OOH or RGB 5,120,255 for DOOH)
  - Flag if container is missing or wrong color

CONTAINER POSITIONING (CRITICAL):
  LANDSCAPE IMAGES:
    - Container MUST be positioned on the RIGHT side of the image
    - DO NOT flag if container is on the right side
    - FLAG ERROR if container is at the top (wrong for landscape)
    - FLAG ERROR if container is on the left side (wrong)

  PORTRAIT IMAGES:
    - Container MUST be positioned at the TOP of the image
    - DO NOT flag if container is at the top
    - FLAG ERROR if container is on the right side (wrong for portrait)
    - FLAG ERROR if container is on the left side (wrong)

CONTAINER SIZING:
  LANDSCAPE IMAGES:
    - Container MUST be 25% of the total width of the advert

  PORTRAIT IMAGES:
    - Container MUST be 25% of the total width of the advert
    - NOTE: For portrait, this refers to the WIDTH (not height) of the container

BORDER SIZING:
  LANDSCAPE IMAGES:
    - Border MUST be 2.5% of the ad size

  PORTRAIT IMAGES:
    - Border MUST be 5% of the width of the advert

BORDER CONTINUITY:
  - Border MUST be continuous with no breaks or interruptions
  - Border MUST NOT have any encroachment or deviation from key art elements
  - Border MUST be one continuous straight line around the key art
  - Flag if any element from the key art breaks through or touches the border (except approved breakouts)

CORNER RADIUS:
  - Corner radius MUST match the border width
  - Example: If border width is 10.5mm, corner radius should be 10.5mm
  - Measure and verify this matches exactly


BREAKOUT REQUIREMENTS:
BREAKOUT SIZE LIMITS:
  - Minimum: Breakout MUST reach at least 50% into the border
  - Maximum: Breakout CANNOT reach more than 75% into the border
  - Flag if breakout is less than 50% or more than 75%

BREAKOUT CLEARANCE (CRITICAL - CHECK CAREFULLY):
  - Breakouts MUST NOT touch, overlap, or come close to the CONTAINER LOGO
  - The container logo is the Prime/Amazon Prime logo INSIDE the blue container at the top
  - Check visually: Is any part of the breakout (head, arm, object, etc.) touching or very close to the container logo?
  - If YES, this is a CRITICAL ERROR - flag immediately
  - "Too close" means within a few pixels or visually appearing to touch
  - Breakouts MUST also maintain clearance from:
    * Title Treatment (TT)
    * CTA (call to action)
    * Signature (logo above title treatment)
  - Flag if breakout is touching, overlapping, or too close to ANY of these elements
  - IMPORTANT: Carefully examine the container area - breakouts frequently encroach here
  - Look at where the breakout extends into the border/container area
  - Measure the distance between the breakout edge and the container logo
  - If the breakout appears to touch or be very close to the logo, FLAG IT

MEASUREMENTS:

  - Example: "Container is 24.8% of total width - does not meet exact 25% requirement"
  - Example: "Container is 25% of total width - complies with requirement"
  - DO NOT ask for verification - make a determination based on measurements
  - Flag ANY deviations from specified exact values

CENTERING AND ALIGNMENT (IMPORTANT - AVOID FALSE POSITIVES):
  - DO NOT FLAG centering issues unless an element is CLEARLY and OBVIOUSLY misaligned
  - If an element looks centered to your eyes, DO NOT FLAG IT
  - Only flag centering if the element is clearly pushed to one side (left-aligned or right-aligned)
  - Acceptable centering tolerance: Elements can be up to ~10% away from perfect center and still be considered "centered"
  - Use professional judgement - if a designer would consider it centered, don't flag it
  - When in doubt, DO NOT FLAG centering issues
  - Better to miss a minor centering issue than to flag a false positive

COLORS:
  - For OOH assets: Check CMYK values match exactly
  - For DOOH assets: Check RGB values match exactly
  - Report actual color values found vs required values

SPELLING AND TYPOS:

  - Example: "SRIES" is NOT the same as "SERIES" - flag this as an error
  - Carefully verify each letter in all text, especially in CTAs and titles

ELEMENT IDENTIFICATION:
  - DO NOT confuse the Signature (above TT) with the CTA (below TT)
  - DO NOT confuse the Container Logo (in container) with the Signature (above TT)
  - Clearly identify each element before checking compliance
